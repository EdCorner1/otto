# Otto Blog Post Template

## Philosophy
Every post should be structured so a reader can:
- **Scan it in 30 seconds** and decide to read
- **Get the key point** even if they only read the first paragraph of each section
- **Take action** because something was clear, specific, and useful

## Standard Post Structure

```
[COVER IMAGE — 16:9 — emotionally resonant, not generic stock]

[HOOK — 2-3 sentences. Start with the controversial or surprising claim.
The reader should feel "yes, that's exactly my problem" or "wait, really?"]

---

## Section 1 — [Problem/Context]  ← H2

[2-4 paragraphs. Enter the reader's world. Use their language.
Specific example: "You send 20 DMs and get 3 replies. 2 of those are other creators.
1 brand replies. They want "exposure" as payment." — real, specific, no fluff.]

[If applicable: Callout Box — a key stat, quote, or data point that reinforces
your argument. Use the > NOTE: or > TIP: syntax.]

### Sub-point  ← H3 (only if this section has distinct sub-themes)

[1-2 paragraphs.]

---

## Section 2 — [Core Insight/Framework]  ← H2

[The main thing you want the reader to带走. This is the payload.
Format: name the framework, explain it in 1 sentence, then unpack it.
Keep it to 3-4 concrete points. No abstract principles — always specific.]

> TIP: [One actionable thing the reader can do RIGHT NOW after reading this]

[Image or visual here if it helps explain the concept — use caption syntax]

---

## Section 3 — [Proof/Example]  ← H2

[Real example. Could be your own experience, a case study, a reader story.
Always: what was the situation → what did they do → what was the result.
Use real numbers. "I charged £150 for this" not "I charged a fair rate."]

---

## Section 4 — [Application/How To]  ← H2

[If this is a practical post: step-by-step, checklist, or template.
If it's an opinion/framework post: what are the 3 scenarios where this applies,
or the 3 mistakes people make when ignoring this advice.]

---

## [Key Takeaways]  ← H2 (optional — use for complex posts)

[3-4 bullet points. Maximum. Reader should be able to screenshot this
and have a useful note even if they forget everything else.]

---

## CTA — Bottom of every post

[One sentence that bridges from the post topic to the signup/community.
Must feel like a natural next step, not a cold sell.]

→ Join [X] readers getting UGC insights that actually convert.
[Link to signup or newsletter]

---

## Related Posts (auto-linked by renderer)

[List 2-3 posts that naturally follow this one.
The renderer will display these at the bottom of the article.]

## Special Markdown Elements

### Callout Boxes
```
> NOTE: [Something important the reader should know]
> TIP:  [One actionable thing they can do right now]
> WARNING: [Common mistake to avoid]
> EXAMPLE: [A concrete example of the concept]
```

### Image with Caption
```
![Alt text](image-url)
^ Photo: Photographer Name / Source — What the reader is looking at
```

### Inline CTA (within article)
```
{{CTA: create your account →}}
```
Renders as a styled inline banner.

### Section Divider
```
---
* * *
---
```
Standard markdown hr.

### Key Quote (styled pull-quote)
```
> "[Exact quote from someone real, with attribution]"
```
Renders as a large styled pull-quote.

## Tone Checklist

- [ ] Hook makes a specific, provable claim (not generic advice)
- [ ] Every H2 answers "what does this help me do?"
- [ ] Real numbers, real names, real scenarios — no "a creator I know"
- [ ] Reading level: 6th grade (Hemingway app test)
- [ ] Sentences ≤14 words
- [ ] No passive voice
- [ ] "You" appears in every section
- [ ] Call to action feels like an invitation, not a demand
