# Otto Blog Content Framework

## Content Pillars

1. **Tech UGC** — creating content about tech products (AI tools, SaaS, gadgets, apps)
2. **Creator Economy** — building a business as a creator, income, sustainability
3. **Brand Partnerships** — landing deals, negotiating, managing client relationships
4. **Tools & Gear** — what actually works for tech creators (editing, audio, lighting, scheduling)
5. **Industry News** — trends, platform changes, what's working right now

---

## The Otto Voice

**What Otto sounds like:**
- Direct. No "in today's digital landscape..."
- Specific. Uses real numbers: "£750 per deal", "2,400 hours per year", "30-day challenge"
- Honest about risk. "Here's what usually goes wrong" alongside "here's what works"
- UK/European context where possible — most UGC advice is US-centric
- Confident but not guru-ish. No "mastermind", no "secret", no "7-figure"

**Anti-patterns (things Otto NEVER writes):**
- Generic "5 tips for better content" without specifics
- "Social media" as a catch-all — always name the platform
- "Authentic" without explanation — what does it actually look like?
- Advice that only works for people with 100K+ followers
- "Build your personal brand" without saying what that actually means

**Sentence style:**
- Average sentence: 12-18 words
- Max sentence: 30 words
- Short punchy sentences for emphasis
- No more than 3 adjectives in a row

## Post Structure (The Otto Template)

Every post follows this skeleton:

### 1. The Hook (first 100 words)
- Opens with a contrarian or surprising statement
- States the problem clearly in one sentence
- No preamble — straight into the issue
- Optional: a specific number that hooks curiosity

Example:
> "Most tech creators are charging about 60% less than they should. Not because they lack talent — because nobody ever told them how to price it. Here's the math."

### 2. What Everyone Else Gets Wrong (1-2 paragraphs)
- Brief context on the conventional/wrong approach
- Why it doesn't work in 2-3 sentences
- Sets up why Otto's approach is different

### 3. The Core Content (the meat)
- 3-6 H2 sections
- Each H2 follows this sub-structure:
  - **Bold lead statement** — the main point in 1 sentence
  - **Explanation** — why this matters, 2-4 sentences
  - **Specific example or number** — a real case, real price, real result
  - **How-to** — the actionable steps (numbered if multiple)

### 4. Key Takeaways Box
- TL;DR section with 3-5 bullet points
- Each bullet: specific, actionable, one-liners
- Placed before or after the main content as a scannable summary

### 5. The CTA
- Not generic "follow for more"
- Related to the content — an offer, a next step, something specific

## Per-Category Writing Rules

### Tech UGC
- Always name specific platforms and tools (Notion, Raycast, Arc, ChatGPT, Perplexity)
- Include the tech stack used if relevant
- Link to tools mentioned with honest mini-reviews
- Talk about what's changing in AI tools this month

### Creator Economy
- Give actual numbers: income breakdowns, time investments, conversion rates
- Be honest about effort/reward — what's realistic for month 1 vs month 12
- Address the "is it too late?" question directly

### Brand Partnerships
- Walk through a specific deal from first DM to payment received
- Include what brands actually look at when reviewing a creator's profile
- Address rejection — how to handle it, when to move on
- Talk about red flags in contracts

### Tools & Gear
- Real budget tiers: "under £100", "£100-500", "£500+"
- What's worth spending more on vs what cheap alternatives work fine
- Specific product names, not "a good microphone"
- What to skip entirely — what's not worth buying

### Industry News
- What changed and when
- Who it affects and how
- What to do about it this week
- Always have an opinion

## Post Formatting Rules

- **H2 headings:** Descriptive, not cute. "The Formula for Pricing UGC" not "The Secret Sauce"
- **Bold lead statements:** Every paragraph under an H2 starts with a bold summary sentence
- **Lists:** Only when there are 3+ distinct items. Max 7 items per list.
- **Code/technical:** Use inline code for tool names, keyboard shortcuts, file names
- **Links:** Only link to things Ed has explicitly approved. No external links without permission.
- **Statistics:** Cite where they come from. If it's a guess, say "in my experience" not "research shows"
- **Images:** Every post should have a cover image. Use Unsplash URLs for initial posts (Ed approves final choices)
- **Excerpt:** 1-2 sentences. No clickbait. The excerpt is a promise — deliver on it.

---

## Publishing Schedule

- **10 posts per day** when batch generating
- **Spread:** 2-3 posts per publishing window (morning 7-9am UK, midday 12-1pm UK, evening 5-7pm UK)
- **Stagger published_at** by at least 4 hours between posts
- **Category distribution** per day: 4 Tech UGC, 2 Brand Partnerships, 2 Creator Economy, 1 Tools & Gear, 1 Industry News

## Sample Generated Post (Example: Pricing)

Title: How to Actually Price Your Tech UGC Work (With Real Numbers)

Hook: "Most tech UGC creators are charging about 60% less than they should. Not because they lack talent — because nobody ever told them how to do the math. Here's exactly how I calculate every UGC rate I charge."

H2 sections:
1. Why Hourly Thinking Will Make You Poor
2. The Simple Formula That Actually Works
3. What Brands Actually Want to Pay For
4. How to Handle the "What's Your Budget?" Question
5. The One Negotiation Move That Keeps Your Rates High

Key Takeaways:
- Never price by the hour — price by outcome
- Base rate = monthly target ÷ 20
- Always add 15-25% platform fee
- Charge extra for usage rights beyond 30 days
- Offer value-adds instead of discounts
